Trade Promotion Management for Consumer-Packaged-Goods

Project Overview

In this project, I constructed predictive models using R to discern and forecast the impact of trade promotions on sales, thereby optimizing promotional strategies for a leading global food brand. The models aimed to provide actionable insights into the dynamics of consumer behavior in response to promotional activities. Simultaneously, a strategic migration was executed, transitioning intermediate storage operations from SAS Grid to AWS S3. This not only resulted in substantial savings on licensing costs but also achieved an impressive 99% data durability, ensuring the integrity and reliability of the stored information. To further enhance the efficiency of the pipeline, I implemented data seasonality analysis using Facebook's Prophet library. This extraction of temporal patterns and trends allowed for a deeper understanding of the underlying sales data, leading to a significant 20% improvement in the accuracy of the promotional impact forecasts. The amalgamation of advanced statistical modeling, cloud migration, and data-driven insights not only optimized the promotional strategies for the global food brand but also demonstrated a proactive approach to resource management and predictive analytics within the dynamic realm of consumer-packaged goods.
