Gesture detection using wearable based accelerometer

Project Overview

Gesture recognition, ni simple terms, is the ability of a computer or machine to interpret human gestures. These could include a wide array of motions such as hand movements, facial expressions, voice commands and the like. Devices such as cameras, microphones or accelerometers are used to detect and capture human gestures and machine learning / Al algorithms are then applied to interpret the data and recognize the gesture. Interpreting human gestures is an important problem and has many applications. For example, researchers at Ben Gurion University have developed a hand-gesture recognition system that enables doctors t o manipulate digital images during surgeries using hand gestures instead of touch screens or computer keyboards.
I engineered a random forest classification algorithm tailored for recognizing smartwatch hand gestures, achieving a 92% accuracy rate. The algorithm was designed to distinguish between various hand gestures effectively, contributing to enhanced user interaction with smartwatches. Additionally, I utilized PyRQA and PyUnicorn to extract the recurrence rate and transitivity from the inertial measurement unit's data, providing insights into the temporal and spatial characteristics of the gestures. This comprehensive approach aimed to improve the accuracy and effectiveness of smartwatch gesture recognition systems.
